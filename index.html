GDP Worldwide

The choropleth map above displays the total GDP for each country around the world in the year 2021 where the gradient of the color indicates the difference in total GDP. The darker the color is, the higher the GDP is. When thinking about the total GDP, it is instinctive to think that country with a larger population will have a higher total GDP. However, in the real situation, the relationship between population growth and GDP growth is controversial.

The chart above shows the total GDP by continent since 1960, we can see from the graph that the total GDP for each continent displays an increasing trend. Among all continents, North America shows the fastest growing rate.

The chart above shows the total Population growth since 1960, we can see from the graph that the total population from the past 60 years for each continent is steadily increasing. Among all continents, East Asia shows the most population.

From both graphs, we can see that although East Asia has the most population, its total GDP doesn't appear to be the highest. Vice versa, North America has a relatively small population but has the highest total GDP. Therefore, when using GDP as an index of ecnomic growth, it's worth mentioning the GDP per Capita.
